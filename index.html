According to the DEP in 2012 NYC water contained 2.2mg/L Fluoride and on average 0.5mg/L

(http://www.nyc.gov/html/dep/pdf/wsstate12.pdf)


The lowest published lethal dose is about 71mg/kg Fluoride for a human.(http://www.sciencelab.com/msds.php?msdsId=9927595)


The average adult weight for humans 20 years and older in 2012 in the USA was


195.5 Lbs (88.67kg) for men and 166.2 Lbs (75.38kg) for women.

(http://www.cdc.gov/nchs/fastats/bodymeas.htm)


That means that an average male weighing 88.67kg would need to ingest 6.296g (71mg*88.76kg=6295.57mg) and the average women weighing 75.38kg would need to ingest 5.352g (71mg*75.38kg=5351.98mg) of fluoride in order to be killed by it.


Convert that to liters of water that one would have to ingest in a short period of time,


6295.57mg/(2.2mg/L) = 2861.62 L (how much water did you drink today?)


Here's a story of fluoride poisoning from public water in Alaska


http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/8259189


It doesn't say how much water they drank, but it does say that the water supply for the people who got sick (nausea, vomiting, headache) had a concentration of 150mg fluoride/L while the control group (people who didn't get sick) water supply had a concentration of 1.1mg/L.


So, while it is true that fluoride is dangerous and can do harm to humans at higher concentrations, the humans of New York with an average of 0.5mg/L in their drinking water have nothing to worry about. And so far that I've gathered no long-term low-dose test has been performed and I can't think of a reason that it should.
