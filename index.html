DON'T FEAR THE FLUORIDE!

According to the DEP in 2012 NYC water contained on average 0.5mg/L fluoride. The lowest published lethal dose is about 71mg/kg fluoride for a human. The average adult weight for humans 20 years and older in 2012 in the USA was 195.5 Lbs 88.67kg for men and 166.2 Lbs 75.38kg for women.

That means that an average male weighing 88.67kg would need to ingest 6.296g (71mg*88.76kg=6295.57mg) and the average woman weighing 75.38kg would need to ingest 5.352g (71mg*75.38kg=5351.98mg) of fluoride in order to be killed by it. Convert that to liters of water that one would have to ingest in a short period of time, 6295.57mg/(0.5mg/L) = 12591.14 L (how much water did you drink today?).

Here's a story of fluoride poisoning from public water in Alaska It doesn't say how much water they drank, but it does say that the water supply for the people who got sick (nausea, vomiting, headache) had a concentration of 150mg fluoride/L while the control group (people who didn't get sick) water supply had a concentration of 1.1mg/L.

So, while it is true that fluoride is dangerous and can do harm to humans at higher concentrations, the humans of New York with an average of 0.5mg/L in their drinking water have nothing to worry about. And so far that I've gathered no long-term low-dose test has been performed and I can't think of a reason that it should.

It is better for the bottled water companies to have you be afraid of drinking tap water, but check out their fluoride content, 0.075-0.17mg/L for poland spring and evian (about the same for most nestle water), 0.8-1.7mg/L for smartwater.

So, is the .4mg/L difference between NYC's tap water and bottled water going to have an enormous or even slight effect on your health?

I highly doubt it.
