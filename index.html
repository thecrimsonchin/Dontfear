DON'T FEAR THE FLUORIDE!
  • | Share
  • | Tweet

According to the DEP in 2012 NYC water contained on average 0.5mg/L fluoride. The lowest published lethal dose is about 71mg/kg fluoride for a human. The average adult weight for humans 20 years and older in 2012 in the USA was 195.5 Lbs 88.67kg for men and 166.2 Lbs 75.38kg for women.

That means that an average male weighing 88.67kg would need to ingest 6.296g (71mg*88.76kg=6295.57mg) and the average woman weighing 75.38kg would need to ingest 5.352g (71mg*75.38kg=5351.98mg) of fluoride in order to be killed by it. Convert that to liters of water that one would have to ingest in a short period of time, 6295.57mg/(0.5mg/L) = 12591.14 L (how much water did you drink today?).

Here's a story of fluoride poisoning from public water in Alaska It doesn't say how much water they drank, but it does say that the water supply for the people who got sick (nausea, vomiting, headache) had a concentration of 150mg fluoride/L while the control group (people who didn't get sick) water supply had a concentration of 1.1mg/L.

So, while it is true that fluoride is dangerous and can do harm to humans at higher concentrations, the humans of New York with an average of 0.5mg/L in their drinking water have nothing to worry about. In fact, there are benefits to having fluoride in your water and that's why it's added, just like iodine is added to table salt. The first US city to have fluoridated water was Grand Rapids, Michigan (1945). Here's a paper reviewing the findings in the eleventh year of the 15 year study that started it all, written by the director of the National Institute of Dental Research at the time. They observed an average 46.5% reduction in tooth decay in children 11-16 years old over the 11 years. At the end of the discussion he writes,

"In all these studies particular attention and interest has been paid to any effects fluoridation might have on other biologic systems of the body. In none of the studies has there been any scientific evidence to suggest an adverse effect on any segment of a rather large population living under divergent environmental conditions. These observations,in conjunction with the fact that millions of people have used naturally fluoridated waters for generations, attest to the complete safety of the procedure."

It is better for the bottled water companies to have you be afraid of drinking tap water, but check out their fluoride content, 0.075-0.17mg/L for poland spring and evian (about the same for most nestle water), 0.8-1.7mg/L for smartwater.

So, is the .4mg/L difference between NYC's tap water and bottled water going to have an enormous or even slight deleterious effect on your health?

I highly doubt it.
